About This Course

About

A world-class graduate course in business research methods, designed for leaders who make decisions grounded in evidence.

Course Overview

Strategic Business Research Methods is a comprehensive, asynchronous graduate course that equips MBA and MSBA students with the methodological foundations needed to design, execute, and evaluate business research. The course bridges the gap between rigorous methodology and practical business application, providing actionable skills for evidence-based decision-making.

Across eight carefully sequenced modules, students progress from understanding why research matters for strategic leadership, through the mechanics of research design, data collection, and analysis, to the art of communicating findings that drive organizational action. Each module integrates real-world business cases, hands-on exercises, and ethical reflection rooted in the Jesuit tradition.

About the Instructor

Vivek H. Patil, Ph.D. is a faculty member at the School of Business Administration, Gonzaga University. With decades of experience teaching marketing research and business analytics at the graduate level, Dr. Patil brings deep expertise in research methodology, survey design, and data analysis to this course. His teaching integrates rigorous academic foundations with the practical realities of business decision-making.

Dr. Patil is also the author of Notes on Survey Design, a comprehensive guide to survey research methodology that serves as a companion resource for several modules in this course.

Jesuit Values in Business Research

As a course offered through Gonzaga University, a Jesuit institution, this material is deeply informed by the Ignatian tradition of intellectual inquiry. We believe that business research is not merely a technical exercise but an ethical practice that carries real consequences for people and communities.

Cura Personalis

Care for the whole person. We design research that respects participants as complete human beings, honoring their dignity and perspectives.

Magis

The relentless pursuit of excellence. We aim for research that is not just competent but outstanding—rigorous, ethical, and meaningful.

Social Responsibility

Research should benefit society. We examine how inquiry can serve communities, reduce harm, and create value for all stakeholders.

Ethical Integrity

Integrity at every stage. Ethical considerations are woven into every phase of the research process, from design to dissemination.

Critical Thinking

Rigorous analytical reasoning. We question assumptions, examine evidence carefully, and draw conclusions that withstand scrutiny.

Global Perspective

Research in a connected world. We account for diverse populations, cultural contexts, and the global implications of business decisions.

Course Design and Pedagogy

This course is designed for asynchronous online delivery over 8 weeks (2 credits). Each module includes:

  • Rich narrative content that reads like a graduate textbook, with concepts explained in depth and context
  • Real-world case studies that connect methodology to strategic business problems
  • Discussion questions that promote critical reflection and peer engagement
  • Recommended readings from foundational and contemporary academic sources
  • Practical exercises that build cumulative research skills
  • Jesuit integration that connects ethical reflection to methodological practice

Scholarly Foundations

The content draws upon a rich body of scholarship in business and marketing research methodology, including:

  • Churchill, G. A., & Iacobucci, D. (2015). Marketing Research: Methodological Foundations. Cengage Learning.
  • Zikmund, W. G., Babin, B. J., Carr, J. C., & Griffin, M. (2013). Business Research Methods (9th ed.). Cengage Learning.
  • Kumar, V., Aaker, D. A., & Day, G. S. (2002). Essentials of Marketing Research. Wiley.
  • Dillman, D. A., Smyth, J. D., & Christian, L. M. (2014). Internet, Phone, Mail, and Mixed-Mode Surveys: The Tailored Design Method (4th ed.). Wiley.
  • Kotler, P. & Keller, K. L. (2016). Marketing Management (15th ed.). Pearson.
  • Creswell, J. W. & Creswell, J. D. (2018). Research Design: Qualitative, Quantitative, and Mixed Methods Approaches (5th ed.). SAGE Publications.
  • McDaniel, C. & Gates, R. (2002). Marketing Research: The Impact of the Internet. South-Western.
  • Groves, R. M., et al. (2009). Survey Methodology (2nd ed.). Wiley.

Additional journal articles, industry reports, and digital resources are recommended throughout each module.
