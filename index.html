Course Modules

Eight carefully sequenced modules that take you from research foundations to data analysis and reporting.

Week 1

The Strategic Role of Research in Business

Why research matters for business leaders and how the research process drives strategic decision-making.

  • Articulate the value of business research
  • Map the stages of the research process
  • Distinguish basic from applied research
 Week 2

Defining Research Problems and Hypotheses

Translating management problems into actionable research objectives, hypotheses, and research requests.

  • Formulate precise research problems
  • Develop testable hypotheses
  • Write a complete research request
 Week 3

Research Design

Choosing among exploratory, descriptive, and causal designs—and understanding threats to validity.

  • Compare three major research design types
  • Evaluate internal and external validity
  • Select the right design for a given problem
 Week 4

Secondary Data and Literature Review

Leveraging existing data sources, evaluating data quality, and conducting systematic literature reviews.

  • Distinguish primary from secondary data
  • Evaluate secondary data quality
  • Conduct a structured literature review
 Week 5

Qualitative Research Methods

Focus groups, depth interviews, observation, projective techniques, and modern qualitative approaches.

  • Design and conduct qualitative studies
  • Select appropriate qualitative techniques
  • Evaluate strengths and limitations of qualitative data
 Week 6

Survey Research and Questionnaire Design

Designing surveys, constructing questionnaires, understanding measurement scales, and minimizing error.

  • Design effective survey instruments
  • Apply measurement and scaling theory
  • Identify and mitigate survey errors
 Week 7

Sampling Design and Data Collection

Probability and non-probability sampling, sample size determination, and data collection strategies.

  • Compare sampling methods and their trade-offs
  • Calculate appropriate sample sizes
  • Manage the data collection process
 Week 8

Data Analysis, Interpretation, and Reporting

Hypothesis testing, key statistical tests, cross-tabulation, and presenting research findings that drive action.

  • Conduct and interpret hypothesis tests
  • Apply chi-square, t-tests, and ANOVA
  • Prepare professional research reports

A Jesuit Approach to Business Research

Grounded in the Ignatian tradition of intellectual rigor, ethical reflection, and service to others.

Cura Personalis

Care for the whole person. In research, this means respecting every participant as a complete human being—not merely a data point—and designing studies that honor their dignity, time, and perspectives.

Magis

The pursuit of excellence and "the greater good." We aspire not merely to competent research but to research that makes a meaningful difference—for organizations, communities, and society.

Ethical Rigor

Jesuit education insists on integrity at every stage. In this course, ethical considerations are not an afterthought but a foundation—woven into problem formulation, data collection, analysis, and reporting.

Men and Women for Others

Business research should serve more than the bottom line. We examine how rigorous inquiry can address societal challenges, reduce harm, and create value for all stakeholders.
